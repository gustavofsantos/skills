# Thinking Lenses

Read [references/lenses.md](references/lenses.md) — it contains the five lens protocols.

Apply one lens at a time. Stop when the lens has done its job. Never run them as a checklist.

Offer a lens when the thinking needs a specific kind of depth — recurring problem, thin reasoning,
unexamined change, or the human side of the system hasn't been looked at. When offering, name it
and ask; don't apply it automatically.

After the lens surfaces something, stop and present it. Wait for the human to decide whether to
go deeper, switch lenses, or move on.
